#### 1. CR-01 Disposition Decision

**Test:** Review `renderScopeBracket` at `extensions/pi-claude-marketplace/shared/notify.ts:683-685`.
The function emits `[<scope>]` whenever `p.scope !== undefined`, but
`docs/messaging-style-guide.md:73` and `docs/output-catalog.md:39` document the orphan-fold rule
(`p.scope !== undefined AND p.scope !== mp.scope`). The catalog UAT passes only because the
`project-orphan-folded` fixture deliberately sets the same-scope plugin row's `p.scope` to avoid
triggering the unimplemented rule. Decide: (a) fix `renderScopeBracket` to accept `mpScope` and
compare (code review CR-01 recommendation), or (b) rewrite the docs to match the implementation
("bracket emits whenever `p.scope` is defined"), or (c) record a follow-up plan for a later phase.

**Expected:** A decision is recorded in planning artifacts (a new plan, an updated CONTEXT.md
decision, or an explicit deferral note in the REVIEW.md). The decision gates whether future
orchestrators (Phase 18-20) can pass `p.scope` through from domain records without producing
spurious scope brackets.

**Why human:** The autoupdate-surface byte forms are correct -- `renderMpHeader` uses `mp.scope`
directly and is unaffected by `renderScopeBracket`. But the pre-existing divergence between the
plugin-row scope-bracket contract and implementation will cause silent mismatch the moment a
Phase 18+ orchestrator constructs a naturally-scoped plugin row. This is a risk assessment and
scheduling decision, not a question automated grep can answer.
